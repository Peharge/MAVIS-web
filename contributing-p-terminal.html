Contributing to P-Terminal

Thank you for your interest in P-Terminal! While we do not currently accept code contributions, we greatly value feedback and suggestions from the community to help improve P-Terminal. Your insights and suggestions will play a key role in making P-Terminal even better for researchers and scientists.

How You Can Help

1. Provide Feedback

  • Share your experience using P-Terminal. What works well? What could be improved?
  • Report any bugs or issues you encounter.
  • Suggest new features or enhancements.

To provide feedback, open a new issue on GitHub. Please include:

  • A clear title summarizing the issue or suggestion.
  • A detailed description, steps to reproduce (if applicable).
  • Any relevant screenshots, logs, or context.

2. Rate and Review P-Terminal

  • If you find it useful, leave a review on platforms where it's available.
  • Share your thoughts via GitHub Issues.

Your feedback helps us prioritize and understand usage in the scientific community.

3. Spread the Word

  • Tell colleagues, researchers, and peers about P-Terminal.
  • Share experiences via social media, blogs, or academic platforms.

Reporting Issues

If you encounter bugs or issues, report them via a new issue in the GitHub Issues section. Include:

  • A detailed description
  • Steps to reproduce the issue
  • Any error messages, logs, or screenshots

Code of Conduct

By participating in the P-Terminal community, you agree to our Code of Conduct. We aim to foster a respectful and supportive environment.

Thank You!

Even though P-Terminal is not currently accepting code contributions, we deeply appreciate your support through feedback, reviews, and spreading the word. Your involvement helps make P-Terminal a better platform for scientists and researchers worldwide.
